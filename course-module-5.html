1. SEO Fundamentals

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of optimizing your website and content to rank higher in search engine results. Unlike paid advertising, SEO is an organic, long-term strategy that builds sustainable traffic.

Why SEO Matters

  • Long-term Traffic: Organic traffic compounds over time
  • Credibility: Users trust organic results more than ads
  • Lower Cost: No per-click fees after initial optimization
  • Competitive Advantage: Rank above competitors
  • Data Insights: Learn what your audience searches for

2. How Search Engines Work

Search engines use complex algorithms to determine which pages best answer a user's query.

The 3 Main Processes:

1. Crawling: Search bots discover new/updated pages

2. Indexing: Pages are analyzed and stored in the index

3. Ranking: Results are ranked based on relevance and authority

3. Keyword Research

Keywords are the foundation of SEO. You need to know what your audience is searching for.

Types of Keywords

  • Head Keywords: Short, high-volume ("digital marketing")
  • Long-tail Keywords: Specific phrases, lower volume, higher intent ("best digital marketing course for beginners")
  • Local Keywords: Include location ("digital marketing agency near me")
  • LSI Keywords: Semantically related terms

Keyword Research Tools

  • Google Keyword Planner: Free, official Google tool
  • Ahrefs: Comprehensive SEO platform
  • SEMrush: Competitor analysis and keywords
  • Ubersuggest: Affordable keyword and content research

4. On-Page SEO

On-page SEO refers to optimizing elements on your web pages for search engines.

Critical On-Page Elements

  • Title Tag: 50-60 characters, include main keyword, compelling
  • Meta Description: 155-160 characters, summarize content, include CTA
  • Heading Tags (H1, H2, H3): Organize content hierarchy, include keywords naturally
  • URL Structure: Short, descriptive, keyword-rich
  • Internal Links: Link to other relevant pages on your site
  • Image Alt Text: Describe images for accessibility and SEO
  • Page Speed: Faster pages rank better

5. Content Optimization

Content must be written for both users and search engines.

Best Practices

  • Include target keyword in first 100 words
  • Use keyword variations naturally throughout
  • Write comprehensive content (2000+ words for competitive topics)
  • Use subheadings to break up content
  • Include related topics (LSI keywords)
  • Write for readability (short sentences, bullet points)

6. AI for SEO Copywriting

Use AI to create SEO-optimized content at scale.

AI SEO Prompt Example

"Write an SEO-optimized blog post about 'digital marketing for small businesses'. Include the keywords 'digital marketing,' 'small business marketing,' and 'online marketing strategies' naturally. The post should be 2000 words, have 5 H2 subheadings, 15 H3 subheadings, include practical tips, and be written for small business owners."

7. Technical SEO

  • Mobile-Friendly: Responsive design essential
  • SSL Certificate: HTTPS is a ranking factor
  • XML Sitemap: Help search engines discover pages
  • Robots.txt: Control which pages get crawled
  • Structured Data: Use schema markup for rich snippets

Key Takeaways

  • SEO is a long-term strategy that builds sustainable traffic
  • Keyword research is the foundation of any SEO strategy
  • Optimize both on-page elements and content for search engines
  • AI can help you create SEO-optimized content faster
  • Technical SEO ensures search engines can crawl and index your site
